My Failed Startups

UX AI – LLM-Based UX Evaluation System

Summary:
  • Explored an AI-driven backend capable of interpreting UI mockups to generate structured, actionable UX feedback.
  • Used prompt engineering, few-shot prompting, and chain-of-thought techniques for enhanced relevance.
  • Designed a UX scoring framework based on design best practices and LLM alignment.
Technologies & Techniques:
  • Prompt Engineering: Crafting detailed instructions to guide LLM responses effectively
  • Few‑Shot & Chain‑of‑Thought Prompting: Techniques that enhance multi-step reasoning in LLM output
  • LLM Alignment & RLHF Concepts: Principles used to align AI feedback with human values and design metrics
Details:
I started building UX_AI as a concept platform where UI designers could upload mockups and receive categorized, LLM-generated feedback. The backend was designed to process screenshots, apply prompt engineering methods—including few-shot examples and chain-of-thought reasoning—and return structured suggestions grounded in UX best practices. Although the foundation was laid for potential implementation, the project did not reach a deployable prototype stage due to time constraints and shifting commitments.
Key Learnings:
  • Gained hands-on experience in crafting effective LLM prompts, especially for UX-specific tasks.
  • Understood the commitment required to operationalize alignment techniques like RLHF in controlled feedback systems.
  • Appreciated the importance of balancing innovation with execution, especially when managing ambitious projects alongside other responsibilities.

Huddl — University Student Forum

Summary:
  • Co-initiated a peer-to-peer forum for IIIT Delhi to bridge communication for juniors seeking academic and campus advice.
  • Worked toward reducing administrative workload by encouraging mentorship and repeated Q&As.
  • Project was started and partly developed, though not completed end-to-end due to evolving priorities and feasibility issues.
Technologies & Tools: ReactJS (frontend), Node.js/Express backend, MongoDB, user authentication logic, QA forum UI design, agile prototyping methodologies.
Details:
Huddl began as a startup-style project aimed at building a campus-first Q&A platform. The idea was to enable juniors to ask questions—academic, administrative, social—while seniors provided credible, reusable answers. I helped craft both frontend and backend components, including user authentication, forum structure, and a straightforward UI. Although we reached early alpha development and initiated promotions, the platform didn’t advance to full deployment. We discovered students were reluctant to pay, even at low pricing tiers, and we lacked the bandwidth to pivot or explore other revenue models.
Key Learnings:
  • Market fit is paramount. Simply having a good idea isn’t enough—user willingness to invest, even minimally, is essential.
  • Feasibility matters. Early-stage projects need realistic timelines and sustained team bandwidth, especially when academic commitments are high.
  • Iterative validation pays off. Starting with prototyping and early feedback could guide pivots before full development—something I’d prioritize in future entrepreneurial efforts.
